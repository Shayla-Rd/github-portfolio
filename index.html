About Me

I am a Mechanical Engineering student at Cornell University with a passion for solving complex problems and creating innovative solutions. My journey began at Eleanor Roosevelt High School, where I participated in the Science and Technology Program. This program sparked my interest in engineering and laid the foundation for my career path.

This program fostered my passion for science and engineering, providing a rigorous foundation in both theoretical knowledge and practical problem-solving. During my junior and senior years, I had the privilege of participating in a research practicum, which allowed me to apply my academic learning to real-world scenarios.

In addition to the practicum, I secured an internship at the Howard University Beltsville Campus, where I worked alongside a team of researchers in the field of environmental science. My primary focus was on studying the flow of particulate matter in Bolivia, a project that involved extensive data collection and analysis. This internship provided me with firsthand experience in environmental research, where I explored the intersection of mechanical engineering and sustainability. It also enhanced my understanding of how engineering solutions can mitigate environmental issues on a global scale.

Through this hands-on research experience, I gained invaluable insights into the challenges of designing systems that promote environmental sustainability. The exposure to real-world applications of mechanical engineering deepened my interest in sustainable engineering practices and reinforced my commitment to finding innovative solutions to environmental problems.

Currently, at Cornell, I am honing my skills as a mechanical engineer, participating in various design projects that challenge me to think critically and creatively. I am passionate about contributing to engineering solutions that make a positive impact on society, and I look forward to further developing my skills in the field.

My Projects

Orange Crusher (CAD Design)

I created a CAD model for an orange crusher that is designed to help users easily crush oranges with minimal effort. This project was a great exercise in learning how to design functional mechanical systems and refine engineering solutions.

Portable Liquid Dispenser

A TSA-approved portable dispenser designed for traveling, optimizing convenience for storing liquids like shampoo and conditioner. This dispenser features a rotating mechanism that is compact and easy to carry.

VEX Robotics - State Competition

As part of a team, I built a robot from scratch for the VEX Robotics competition in high school. Our robot successfully reached the state competition, demonstrating our skills in mechanical design and programming.

Toy Car Design

In my freshman year of college, I worked with a group to design and build a small toy car from scratch. This project taught me the importance of teamwork, prototyping, and testing, as well as refining mechanical designs to meet specific goals.
